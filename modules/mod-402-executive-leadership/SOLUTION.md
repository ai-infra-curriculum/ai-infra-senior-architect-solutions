# SOLUTION — Executive Leadership

> Read this *after* you have attempted the executive-leadership
> deliverables. This document explains the senior-architect-tier
> lens on operating at the executive level.

## What this module is really teaching

At the senior-architect altitude, the audience is the C-suite.
The skills:
- Speaking the language of business outcomes, not technical
  outcomes.
- Influencing without authority across business units.
- Setting technical vision that survives leadership changes.
- Managing senior engineering leaders (directors, VPs).

## What the deliverables should actually look like

### Technical-vision narrative (exercise 01)

A 2-page narrative describing the technical north star for the
next 3-5 years. Written in business-outcome language, with
technical detail as supporting evidence.

### Executive influence map (exercise 02)

Per executive: their priorities, what they need from you, what
you need from them, the relationship cadence (1-1 frequency).

### Board pre-read (exercise 03)

Quarterly board package: AI strategy progress, infrastructure
investments, risks, asks. 10-15 pages.

### Crisis communication template (exercise 04)

When something significant goes wrong, the senior architect
communicates: what happened, what was the impact, what's the
recovery, what changes prevent recurrence.

### Succession plan (exercise 05)

Who's ready for what role at what timeline. Identifying high-
potential senior engineers and architects + their development
paths.

## Trade-offs we deliberately accepted

- Executive language flattens technical nuance.
- Influence without authority is slow.
- Board-level communication is heavily edited.

## Common mistakes graders see

1. **Technical-vision document full of jargon**: executives
   tune out.
2. **No succession plan**: bus factor of 1.
3. **Crisis comms that minimize impact**: executives
   eventually find out.
4. **Influence map filled out once, never updated**.

## When to go beyond this implementation
