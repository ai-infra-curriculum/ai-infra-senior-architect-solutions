# SOLUTION — Innovation and R&D

> Read this *after* you have attempted the innovation
> deliverables. This document explains the senior-architect-tier
> view of organizational R&D.

## What this module is really teaching

Most established companies fail at innovation not for lack of
budget but for lack of structure. The senior architect designs:
- R&D portfolio with diverse risk profiles.
- Stage gates that kill projects honestly.
- Connection between research and production engineering.
- Talent allocation to innovation vs. operational work.

## What the deliverables should actually look like

### R&D portfolio document (exercise 01)

Horizon-1 (incremental), Horizon-2 (adjacent), Horizon-3
(transformative) bets sized appropriately. Most resources on
H1; meaningful chunks on H2; small focused on H3.

### Stage-gate framework (exercise 02)

Discovery -> Validation -> Scaling stages with kill criteria at
each. The framework's value is in honestly killing weak
projects, not in approving strong ones.

### Research-to-production handoff (exercise 03)

How research prototypes become production systems: which team
owns the transition, what the rebuild expectations are, how
research insights survive engineering rewrites.

### Innovation talent strategy (exercise 04)

Who works on H1 vs. H2 vs. H3? How do they rotate? How does the
org avoid the "innovation team isolated from real engineering"
failure mode?

### Patent / IP strategy (exercise 05)

Defensive vs. offensive IP strategy. Open-source policy.
Academic publication policy.

## Trade-offs we deliberately accepted

- Innovation is expensive; most bets fail.
- Stage gates are politically uncomfortable to enforce.
- Research-to-production transitions lose ~half the
  innovation in translation.

## Common mistakes graders see

1. **Innovation portfolio all H1**: incremental forever.
2. **Stage gates with no kill criteria**.
3. **Research teams isolated**: no engineering pull-through.
4. **No IP strategy**: defaults to chaotic.

## When to go beyond this implementation

- Read **The Innovator's Dilemma** (Christensen).
- Study **DARPA-style program management** for structured
  high-risk research.
