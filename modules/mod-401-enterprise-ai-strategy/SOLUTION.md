# SOLUTION — Enterprise AI Strategy

> Read this *after* you have attempted the strategy deliverables.
> The "solutions" here are senior-architect-tier rubrics.

## What this module is really teaching

Senior architects sit at the boundary between engineering and
executive strategy. The AI-strategy skill set is:
- Translating business strategy into AI capability investments.
- Picking which AI bets the company should make.
- Sequencing the bets across multi-year horizons.
- Defending the bets to the board.

## What the deliverables should actually look like

### AI strategy document (exercise 01)

5-10 page document for an executive audience. Includes:
- The business thesis the AI strategy supports.
- The 3-5 specific capabilities being invested in.
- The 18-36 month roadmap.
- The investment ask and expected return.

### Bet portfolio (exercise 02)

Multiple bets sized by capital and timeline, with risk
diversification. Including some high-risk / high-payoff bets
alongside the safe ones.

### Capability investment plan (exercise 03)

Build / buy / partner decision per capability, with TCO and
strategic-value analysis.

### Risk register (exercise 04)

Strategic risks ranked by likelihood + impact: regulatory,
talent, competitive, technology. Mitigations per top-tier risk.

### Board-level presentation (exercise 05)

10-15 slide deck. Lead with revenue / strategic implication.
Engineering detail in appendix.

## Trade-offs we deliberately accepted

- Strategy at this altitude is forecasting; most bets fail
  partially.
- Executive consumers want certainty; architects must
  communicate honest uncertainty.

## Common mistakes graders see

1. **AI strategy = "we will use AI"**: no concrete bets.
2. **Single-bet portfolio**: fragile if the bet fails.
3. **No kill criteria**.
4. **Board deck with technical detail**.

## When to go beyond this implementation

- Read CEO letters at AI-leading companies (Microsoft,
  Anthropic, OpenAI) for narrative patterns.
- Study **technology adoption lifecycle** (Geoffrey Moore).
